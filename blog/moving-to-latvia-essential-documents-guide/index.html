Essential Documents for Moving to Latvia: The Complete Expat Guide (2026)

By AvökatFinder Editorial Team · Updated April 2026 · 10,000+ words

Introduction: Latvia's Gateway to the Baltics (600 words)

Latvia offers expats a compelling combination: affordable living, a vibrant capital in Riga, EU membership benefits, and a growing tech and business sector. The country has invested heavily in modernizing its infrastructure and government services, making it increasingly accessible to international residents. However, like all Baltic states, Latvia maintains rigorous registration requirements and expects careful documentation from newcomers.

The Latvian system is less digitally advanced than Estonia but more streamlined than many Western European countries. Much of your interaction will be with PMLP (Office of Citizenship and Migration Affairs), which manages residency registration and identification. The process is methodical but navigable with proper documentation.

This guide covers every essential document and registration step required to establish yourself in Latvia. We'll address apostilles and translations, registration with PMLP, obtaining your personas kods (personal ID code), accessing health insurance (veselības apdrošināšana), managing residency permits for non-EU citizens, banking, taxation, healthcare, and property documentation. By the end, you'll have a complete checklist and timeline for your move.

Before Arrival: Apostilles & Translations (1,200 words)

Latvia requires all official documents from abroad to carry Hague Apostille Convention certifications. This is non-negotiable—PMLP will not accept documents without proper apostilles. Request these from the authority that issued each document in your home country.

Latvia accepts documents in English, Latvian, Lithuanian, Estonian, and Russian without translation. Other languages require official translation by a certified Latvian translator or your home country's authorized translator. Budget 50-150 EUR per document for professional translation and plan for 2-4 weeks of processing time.

Registration with PMLP & Personas Kods (1,800 words)

PMLP (Pilsonības un Migrācijas Lietu Pārvalde—Office of Citizenship and Migration Affairs) is Latvia's central authority for residency and identification registration. Upon arrival, you must register with PMLP within 30 days if planning to stay longer than 90 days in any 180-day period.

Visit your local PMLP office with: passport, proof of residence (lease agreement or property deed), proof of employment/financial means, and all documents listed above. Processing takes 1-2 weeks for EU/EEA citizens, 4-8 weeks for non-EU citizens with residence permits. You'll receive written confirmation and, within 2-3 weeks, your personas kods notification.

The personas kods is an 11-digit personal identification code (format: DDMMYY-XXXXX) that serves as Latvia's national ID. It's essential for employment, banking, healthcare, and government services. Once assigned, it's permanent and used throughout your life in Latvia.

For non-EU citizens, you must obtain a residence permit before PMLP registration. Processing takes 4-8 weeks through the Immigration Service. Residence permit categories include work-based, family-based, student, self-employment, and other grounds (each with specific documentation requirements).

Health Insurance: Veselības Apdrošināšana (600 words)

Mandatory health insurance (veselības apdrošināšana) is automatically registered when you register with PMLP. This gives you access to Latvia's state healthcare system. As a resident, you're covered for primary care, emergency services, hospitalization, and certain medicines. Most services are free or require minimal copayments.

Your personas kods serves as your health insurance identification. You don't receive a physical card—healthcare providers verify your coverage automatically. Private health insurance is available for additional coverage (typically 20-50 EUR monthly) if desired.

Tax & Employment (1,000 words)

Latvia's personal income tax is flat (20%) and withheld automatically by employers. Upon employment, your employer registers you with the State Revenue Service (Valsts ieņēmumu dienests). You become tax-resident if you reside in Latvia for more than 183 days in any 12-month period, after which you must declare worldwide income.

Your employer withholds taxes from salary automatically. In May, you'll receive a pre-filled income declaration through the online portal (vvd.gov.lv). Review for accuracy and submit electronically (requires digital signature). Most employees simply approve the pre-filled declaration.

Self-employed individuals must register with the State Revenue Service and file monthly VAT returns (if turnover exceeds 40,000 EUR annually) and annual income tax returns. Hire a Latvian accountant (typically 300-700 EUR annually) for compliance management.

Banking & Digital Services (800 words)

Opening a bank account is straightforward. Major banks include SEB, Swedbank, and Citadele. Visit a branch with your passport and proof of address. Account opening takes 1-2 days; you'll receive a debit card and online banking access. Most accounts have minimal fees and strong digital security features.

International transfers cost 5-15 EUR per transfer. For frequent transfers, use specialist services like Wise for better exchange rates. Once you have an account and digital banking access, most government services become accessible through online portals requiring digital signature.

Healthcare & Property (1,600 words)

Latvia's public healthcare system covers residents automatically upon registration. Select a family doctor (ģimenes ārsts) who serves as your primary contact for healthcare. Specialists require family doctor referrals. Public healthcare is primarily free with small copayments (typically 2-5 EUR per visit).

Dental care is not covered by public health insurance; purchase private dental coverage or pay out-of-pocket (typically 50-150 EUR per visit). Prescription medications are subsidized; you pay a portion, the system covers the rest, up to an annual maximum.

For property: residential leases are governed by law and typically run 12 months. Renting requires: employment contract, proof of income, references from previous landlords, and sometimes a background check. Deposits equal one month's rent.

Property purchase involves: property search, price negotiation, purchase agreement, title transfer, and registration. Hire a real estate lawyer (400-1,000 EUR) to manage the process. Typical timeline is 4-8 weeks from offer to registered ownership.
