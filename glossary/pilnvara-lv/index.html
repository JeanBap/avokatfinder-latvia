Pilnvara (Power of Attorney)

A Latvian pilnvara (power of attorney) allows an appointed representative to complete a property transaction on the buyer's or seller's behalf. For land register purposes, the pilnvara must be notarially certified.

A pilnvara (power of attorney) is used in Latvian property transactions when the buyer or seller cannot be physically present for signing. The pilnvara authorises a named representative (pilnvarnieks — attorney-in-fact) to execute the purchase agreement, submit land register applications, and perform all related acts on the principal's behalf.

For land register purposes, the pilnvara must be certified by a Latvian zvnars (sworn notary) or, if executed abroad, by a foreign notary with an apostille (Hague Convention apostille). The apostilled POA must also be translated into Latvian by a certified translator.

The pilnvara should be narrowly drafted — specifying the exact property (by kadastra numurs and address), the maximum purchase price, and the specific acts the pilnvarnieks is authorised to perform. A broadly drafted POA creates risk that the representative could bind the principal to transactions beyond the intended scope.

Pilnvaras expire after a specified period or upon the principal's death. Latvian law requires the principal's capacity at the time of signing — a pilnvara signed by someone who lacks legal capacity is void. For elderly or incapacitated principals, guardianship (aizgadniba) provisions may apply instead.

In practice, foreign buyers routinely use pilnvaras to appoint Latvian lawyers to act on their behalf throughout the purchase process — from signing the preliminary agreement through to land register submission. This is a well-established and reliable mechanism.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Issuing a broadly worded pilnvara 'to deal with all matters related to real estate in Latvia' gives your attorney-in-fact excessive powers. If misused, recovering from an unauthorised transaction is extremely difficult.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Specify in the pilnvara the maximum purchase price you authorise and the exact property address and kadastra numurs. This ensures the pilnvarnieks cannot bind you to a higher price or a different property.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I revoke a pilnvara?

Yes. A pilnvara can be revoked at any time by the principal. Revocation is effective from the moment the pilnvarnieks is notified. For land register purposes, revocation should be notified to the zvnars or lawyer who holds the pilnvara.

What happens if my pilnvarnieks acts outside the scope of the pilnvara?

Unauthorised acts by the pilnvarnieks can be challenged in court. However, a third party who dealt in good faith with the pilnvarnieks and had no reason to doubt authority may be protected. This is why narrow drafting matters.

Does the apostille replace a Latvian notary certification?

Yes. An apostilled foreign notarial document is accepted by the Latvian zemesgramata without further notarisation in Latvia, provided a certified Latvian translation accompanies it.
