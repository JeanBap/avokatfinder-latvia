Hipotēka

Hipotēka is the Latvian term for a mortgage — a security interest over real property registered in the Zemesgrāmata, giving the lender priority rights against the property in the event of borrower default.

In Latvia, a hipotēka is created by a notarially certified agreement and registered in the Zemesgrāmata as a real right (lietu tiesība). The registration gives the lender priority over the mortgaged property and enables enforcement through public auction if the borrower defaults.

Latvian mortgage law distinguishes between a hipotēka (lender-held charge) and a komercķīla (commercial pledge over moveable assets). For real estate financing, hipotēka is always the appropriate instrument. The Zemesgrāmata records the amount, interest rate cap, and term of each hipotēka.

When purchasing Latvian property encumbered by an existing hipotēka, the purchase contract must include provisions for discharge from the closing proceeds. The buyer's notary coordinates with the lender to ensure simultaneous repayment and a dzēšanas apliecinājums (discharge certificate) is filed.

The Financial and Capital Market Commission (FKTK) regulates Latvian mortgage lenders. Latvian banks offer both fixed and variable rate hipotēka products, with variable rates typically linked to EURIBOR. The maximum LTV ratio for residential purchases is generally 90% (85% for non-residents).

Common Mistake: Completing a Latvian property purchase without confirming that the seller's hipotēka is discharged at closing. A hipotēka is a real right attached to the property — it follows the land, not the person, and binds any new owner.
Expert Tip: Structure the closing payment so that the amount required to discharge the hipotēka is paid directly to the lender, with the dzēšanas apliecinājums filed at the Zemesgrāmata simultaneously before the balance is released to the seller.

After full repayment of a Latvian hipotēka, request the dzēšanas apliecinājums from the lender immediately and file for removal from the Zemesgrāmata. An uncleared hipotēka complicates any future sale or remortgage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a dzēšanas apliecinājums in Latvia?

A dzēšanas apliecinājums is a discharge certificate issued by the Latvian mortgage lender confirming the hipotēka debt has been repaid. It is submitted to the Zemesgrāmata to remove the charge from the property's land book entry.

How is a hipotēka enforced in Latvia?

Enforcement of a Latvian hipotēka typically proceeds through a public auction (izsole) administered by a court bailiff (zvērināts tiesu izpildītājs). Out-of-court enforcement through voluntary auction is also permitted under Latvian law.

Can a foreign bank hold a hipotēka on Latvian property?

Yes, subject to the requirements of Latvian law. The hipotēka agreement must be notarially certified by a Latvian zvērināts notārs and registered in the Zemesgrāmata to be effective as a real right against third parties.
