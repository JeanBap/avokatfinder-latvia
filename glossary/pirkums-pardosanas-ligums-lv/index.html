Pirkuma-Pardosanas Ligums (Purchase Agreement)

The Latvian pirkuma-pardosanas ligums is the binding purchase contract for real property. For registration purposes, it must be certified by a licensed zvnars (notary) or sworn notary and submitted to the zemesgramata.

Latvia's property purchase agreement (pirkuma-pardosanas ligums) must be in writing and, for submission to the zemesgramata, must be certified by a zvnars (sworn notary) or an advokats (licensed lawyer) who has the right to certify documents for land register purposes. Simple private contracts — even if signed by both parties — are not accepted by the zemesgramata without certification.

The agreement must identify the property precisely by its kadastra numurs and zemesgramata entry number (nodalijuma numurs). It must state the agreed price, the payment terms, and the parties' details including personal identity numbers (personas kods) for individuals or registration numbers for companies.

A preliminary agreement (rokasnaudas ligums — earnest money agreement) is commonly used for residential purchases before the main pirkuma-pardosanas ligums is signed. The rokasnaudas (deposit) is typically 5–10% and is forfeited if the buyer withdraws, or doubled and returned to the buyer if the seller withdraws.

Latvia does not require a notary for standard residential purchases (unlike some EU countries) — a lawyer with land-register certification rights can certify the agreement. This typically reduces costs compared to full notarial execution.

Foreign buyers should note that Latvian purchase agreements are in Latvian. Translations into English or other languages have no legal force — the Latvian original governs. Ensure you understand the Latvian text (or have a certified translator present) before signing.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Signing a Latvian purchase agreement without a certified Latvian translation into your language — or without a qualified interpreter present — creates a legal risk that you did not understand what you were agreeing to.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For the rokasnaudas (deposit), use a lawyer's escrow account rather than paying directly to the seller. If the seller withdraws, recovering the doubled deposit from a regulated account is far easier than chasing the seller personally.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the nodalijuma numurs?

The zemesgramata section number — the unique identifier for a property's entry in the land register. Every property has one, and it appears on zemesgramata extracts. Always record this number for your property.

Is the rokasnaudas refundable if I change my mind?

No. The rokasnaudas (deposit) is forfeited if the buyer withdraws without legal grounds. This is the standard rule under Latvian civil law unless the agreement explicitly states otherwise.

Can I use an English-language purchase agreement in Latvia?

The zemesgramata requires a Latvian-language certified agreement. English agreements have no status in Latvian land registration. If your lawyer provides an English version, it is for your information only — the Latvian text controls.
