Apgruntajums

Apgruntajums is the Latvian term for an easement or real burden — a registered right over immovable property that limits the owner's use of their land in favour of another property or a specific person.

In Latvian property law, an apgruntajums (easement or real burden) is a registered encumbrance in the Zemesgramata that restricts how the burdened property (piekrautais nekustamais ipashums) may be used, or grants a specific right to the benefiting property (dominejoshais ipashums) or person.

Common types of apgruntajums include: rights of way (cela servituts) over a neighbour's land; utility easements allowing pipelines, cables, or drainage infrastructure to cross a plot; and personal easements (personiskas ealvieglojumi) such as the right to live in a property (uztura ligums) registered in favour of a specific individual.

An apgruntajums is registered in Part III of the Zemesgramata extract. Buyers must examine Part III carefully before purchase. Easements run with the land and bind all future owners — they do not end when ownership changes. An unexamined easement can significantly restrict development or use of the property.

The right of way easement (servituts) is particularly common in rural Latvia, where many plots lack direct road access and rely on a right of passage over a neighbour's land. The width, permitted uses, and maintenance obligations for the path should be specified in the registered document.

An apgruntajums may be created by agreement between landowners (registered by notarial deed), by court order, or by operation of law. It can be removed only by agreement of all affected parties, court judgment, or if the conditions for which it was created no longer exist. Buyers should verify the basis and permanence of any registered easement before purchase.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes ignore Part III of the Zemesgramata extract, focusing only on ownership and mortgages in Parts I and II. A registered right of way or utility easement over a development plot can fundamentally change what can be built and where — always read the full extract.
💡 Expert TipFor plots intended for development, have a surveyor map any registered easements against the actual land boundaries. A right of way that runs diagonally across a plot can force building footprints into unfavourable positions. Know this before purchasing, not after.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I negotiate a release of an apgruntajums from a neighbour?

Yes, if the easement was created by agreement. The benefiting party must consent to its removal, and the release must be notarially authenticated and registered in the Zemesgramata to be effective.

Does an apgruntajums reduce the property's value?

It depends on the type. A utility pipeline easement in a field has minimal impact; a wide right of way across a garden significantly affects usable area. A valuer should factor registered easements into any appraisal.

Can a buyer claim they did not know about a registered apgruntajums?

No. The Zemesgramata is a public register and the principle of public notice applies. Registered encumbrances are deemed known to buyers regardless of whether they actually checked.
