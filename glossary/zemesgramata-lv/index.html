Zemesgramata (Land Register)

Latvia's zemesgramata is the official land register maintained by district courts, recording all property rights, mortgages, and encumbrances. Registration is constitutive — ownership only passes when recorded in the register.

Latvia's zemesgramata (land book) is one of Europe's oldest continuously operating land register systems, with roots in the 19th century. Maintained by district courts (rajonu tiesas), it records all real property rights — ownership, mortgages (hipoteka), easements (servituts), pre-emption rights, and restrictions. The register is organised by property, with each entry identified by a kadastra numurs (cadastral number).

Latvia follows the constitutive registration principle: ownership does not transfer at the time the purchase agreement is signed or notarised, but only when the entry is made in the zemesgramata. Until registration, the buyer has a contractual right against the seller but no property right against the world. This makes prompt registration critical.

Zemesgramata extracts are publicly available online at zemesgramata.lv. The system shows the current owner, registered mortgages, and restrictions. Certified extracts can be ordered from the court registry office for a small fee and are required by banks for mortgage applications.

Transfer applications are submitted to the district court responsible for the area where the property is located. Processing time is typically five working days for standard residential transfers. An expedited service (3 working days) is available for an additional fee.

Stamp duty (kancelejas nodeva) for land register entries is calculated as a percentage of the transaction value — typically 2% for ownership transfers — plus a fixed court fee. The total registration cost is modest relative to transaction value.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not registering promptly after signing means the seller could, in theory, sell to a third party — and that party's prior registration would prevail over your unregistered purchase. Register within days of signing, not weeks.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Use zemesgramata.lv to check the register on the morning of signing — not just the week before. A new mortgage or restriction registered overnight would appear there and should delay your completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the kadastra numurs?

The cadastral number uniquely identifying each property in Latvia's State Cadastre (Kadastra informacijas sistemas — KIS). Every zemesgramata entry references the kadastra numurs for the property.

Can I search the zemesgramata in English?

The portal is in Latvian, but most fields are navigable with a translation tool. Key fields: ipasnieks (owner), hipoteka (mortgage), servituts (easement), aizliegums (prohibition).

Who submits the registration application?

Typically the notary (zvnars) who executed the purchase agreement, or a lawyer. The application includes the signed purchase deed and proof of transfer tax payment.
