Ipasuma Nodoshana

Ipasuma nodoshana is the formal transfer of property ownership in Latvia, a legally structured process that culminates in the new owner being entered in the Zemesgramata (Land Register) as the registered proprietor.

In Latvia, legal ownership of immovable property is established by registration in the Zemesgramata (Land Register), maintained by the district and city courts. The process of ipasuma nodoshana (transfer of ownership) is completed only when the Land Register court enters the new owner's name following a notarially authenticated sale contract (pirkuma-pardoshanas ligums).

The transfer process begins with a notarially authenticated purchase agreement. Latvian law requires that sale agreements for immovable property be executed before a notary (zvererinats notars). Both parties must appear in person or via notarially certified power of attorney. The notary verifies identities, title, and encumbrances before authentication.

After signing, the notary submits a petition (liigums) to the relevant district or city court Land Register section. The court reviews the documentation and enters the new owner in the Zemesgramata. Registration typically takes 5–10 working days under the standard procedure; expedited processing is available for an additional fee.

Between signing the contract and registration, the buyer has an unregistered beneficial interest. During this window, it is theoretically possible for the seller to encumber the property — though in practice the notary's submission and court processing period is short. Buyers should minimise this window by requesting expedited processing.

The land register entry constitutes absolute proof of ownership in Latvia. Once registered, the buyer's title is protected against subsequent claims, subject to the good faith purchaser doctrine. Buyers who register in good faith are protected even if earlier in the chain there were irregularities, provided they had no knowledge of them.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes pay the full purchase price before the Zemesgramata entry is made, assuming the signed contract is sufficient protection. Until registration, the buyer does not have legal title. Structure payment via notarial escrow released simultaneously with registration.
💡 Expert TipRequest expedited Land Register processing (paatrinata registracija) when buying in competitive markets. The additional fee is modest and significantly reduces the window between signing and having confirmed legal title.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I check Zemesgramata status online?

Yes. The Land Register portal (zemesgramata.lv) provides public online access to property records, encumbrances, and ownership history for a small fee.

Does Latvia use a notary escrow for purchase price payment?

Notarial escrow (notara depozits) is available and recommended. Funds are released to the seller only when the court confirms registration of the buyer's title.

What happens if the seller has a mortgage at the time of sale?

The seller's mortgage must be discharged before or simultaneously with the transfer. The notary structures the transaction so that part of the purchase price is used to settle the mortgage, and the bank issues a release simultaneously with the Land Register entry.
