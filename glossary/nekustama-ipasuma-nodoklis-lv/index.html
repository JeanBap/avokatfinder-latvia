Nekustama Ipasuma Nodoklis (Real Estate Tax)

Latvia's nekustama ipasuma nodoklis (NIN) is an annual property tax levied by municipalities on the cadastral value of land and buildings. Rates vary by municipality and property type.

Latvia's real estate tax (nekustama ipasuma nodoklis — NIN) is an annual municipal tax on the cadastral value of immovable property. Rates are set by each municipality within the statutory bands: 0.2–3% for land; 0.2–0.6% for residential buildings; 1.5% for third-party occupied residential buildings (i.e. not owner-occupied). Commercial property rates are higher.

The tax is assessed on the kadastrala vertiba (cadastral value) maintained by the State Cadastre (VZD — Valsts Zemes Dienests). Unlike market value, the cadastral value is updated by VZD periodically and is used as the NIN base. Historically, Latvian cadastral values have been significantly below market values, producing relatively low tax bills.

NIN is assessed annually and invoiced to the owner registered in the zemesgramata as of 1 January. The owner pays the tax in four equal instalments. Non-payment of NIN creates a registered tax debt (nodokla parads) against the property — a new buyer may inherit this debt if not identified and discharged before purchase.

Owner-occupied residential property benefits from a reduced rate if the owner has declared the property as their declared residence (deklareta dzivesvieta). This declaration must be made with the local municipality to qualify for the reduced rate.

Latvia has introduced significant increases in cadastral valuations in recent years as part of a broader tax reform. Buyers should check the current cadastral value and resulting NIN liability before purchase, not rely on the seller's previous bills.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not checking for unpaid NIN debts before purchase means you may inherit the previous owner's tax arrears as a charge on the property. Request a NIN debt certificate from the local municipality before exchange.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Check the current cadastral value on the VZD portal (kadastrs.lv) and use the municipality's NIN calculator to estimate your annual liability. Latvian cadastral revaluations in 2023–2024 produced significant increases for some properties.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the deklareta dzivesvieta?

The declared place of residence — the address you formally register with the municipality as your primary home. This declaration reduces your NIN rate for that property to the owner-occupied band.

Can I appeal my property's cadastral value?

Yes. VZD has an appeal process if you believe the cadastral value is incorrect. You must submit a request within the statutory period after notification of the new assessed value.

Is NIN the same across all Latvian municipalities?

No. Rates and administrative procedures vary by municipality. Riga applies different rates from rural municipalities. Always check with the specific municipality for the applicable rates on your property.
