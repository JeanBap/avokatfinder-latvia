Pirkuma Līgums

A pirkuma līgums is the Latvian sale and purchase agreement — the written contract transferring ownership of real estate from seller to buyer in Latvia, requiring notarial certification for Land Register submission.

The pirkuma līgums (purchase agreement) is the central document in any Latvian real estate transaction. Latvian law requires the agreement to be notarially certified (notariāli apliecināts) for the transfer of real property rights to be registerable in the Land Book (Zemesgrāmata). Without notarial certification, the transfer cannot be registered.

The agreement must precisely identify both parties with their personal identification codes (personas kods), describe the property using its cadastral designation, state the purchase price, set out the payment schedule, and include any conditions. The notary verifies the parties' identities and the legal capacity to transact before certifying.

Latvian law imposes a compulsory first refusal right (pirmpirkuma tiesības) in certain circumstances — co-owners, municipalities, and the state may have priority purchase rights that must be waived before the sale proceeds to a third party. The notary checks for these rights and obtains the necessary waivers.

The state fee (valsts nodeva) for registering a transfer in the Zemesgrāmata is 2% of the property value (capped at €30,000). This is payable on submission and is the buyer's responsibility unless contractually agreed otherwise.

Common Mistake: Assuming the pirkuma līgums alone transfers ownership. In Latvia, ownership only vests on registration in the Zemesgrāmata — the signed and notarially certified contract is necessary but not sufficient to complete the legal transfer.
Expert Tip: Ask your notary to run a Zemesgrāmata search and a cadastre check on the same day as the signing appointment. Both records can change overnight and a same-day search reveals any last-minute encumbrances.

Foreign buyers in Latvia should engage a bilingual Latvian lawyer or notary, as all official documents are in Latvian. The notary can prepare a certified translation, but having independent legal advice in your own language is strongly advisable for complex transactions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a notary required for Latvian property purchases?

Yes. A pirkuma līgums for real property must be notarially certified by a Latvian zvērināts notārs (sworn notary) before it can be submitted to the Zemesgrāmata for registration. An uncertified agreement cannot effect a legal transfer.

What is the cost of registering a Latvian property transfer?

The state fee (valsts nodeva) for Zemesgrāmata registration is 2% of the declared property value, subject to a maximum of €30,000. Additional notarial fees and any applicable stamp duty also apply.

What is pirmpirkuma tiesības in Latvia?

Pirmpirkuma tiesības is the Latvian right of first refusal — a statutory priority purchase right held in certain circumstances by co-owners, neighbouring landowners, the municipality, or the state. All holders must waive this right before the property can be sold to a third party.
