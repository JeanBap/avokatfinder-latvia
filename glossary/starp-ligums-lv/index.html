Starp Ligums (Preliminary Agreement)

The Latvian starp ligums or rokasnaudas ligums is the binding preliminary purchase agreement, typically accompanied by a deposit of 5–10%. Both buyer and seller are bound upon signature, with penalty clauses for non-completion.

Before a formal pirkuma-pardosanas ligums (purchase agreement) is executed, Latvian property buyers and sellers frequently sign a starp ligums (intermediate agreement) or rokasnaudas ligums (earnest money agreement). This preliminary contract commits both parties to complete the transaction on agreed terms within a specified period.

The rokasnaudas (earnest money / deposit) paid at preliminary agreement stage is typically 5–10% of the agreed purchase price. Under Latvian civil law, if the buyer withdraws without legal grounds, the deposit is forfeited. If the seller withdraws, they must return double the deposit (rokasnaudas dubulta apmaksana). These rules apply automatically unless the agreement explicitly modifies them.

Conditions precedent (nosakumi) can be included in the starp ligums — for example, conditional on mortgage approval, planning check, or satisfactory condition survey. If a condition is not met within the agreed timeframe, the party benefiting from the condition can withdraw without penalty, and the deposit is returned.

The starp ligums is a private contract between the parties and is not normally submitted to the zemesgramata. However, for large-value or long-duration preliminary agreements, registration of a claim annotation (atsauces iezime) in the zemesgramata can protect the buyer's priority against subsequent registrations.

Foreign buyers should have the starp ligums reviewed by a Latvian lawyer before signing — even a seemingly straightforward preliminary agreement can contain unfavourable conditions on withdrawal, time limits, and deposit forfeiture that are not immediately obvious in translation.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Signing a starp ligums before your mortgage is approved — without including a financing condition — commits you to the purchase even if the bank declines. Always include an explicit rahoitusehto-equivalent condition.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For a preliminary agreement lasting more than four weeks, ask your lawyer to register an annotation in the zemesgramata protecting your priority. If the seller registers a mortgage or second sale during the period, your annotation takes precedence.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the starp ligums enforceable in court?

Yes. Both parties can be ordered by a Latvian court to perform the main purchase agreement, or can claim damages if the other party refuses. The preliminary agreement creates binding obligations.

What is the typical time between starp ligums and main agreement?

For standard residential purchases, typically 4–12 weeks — enough time to arrange financing, complete due diligence, and prepare the final agreement. Longer periods are used for off-plan or complex transactions.

Can conditions precedent be added after the starp ligums is signed?

No. Conditions must be agreed and included when the preliminary agreement is signed. Subsequent amendments require a written addendum signed by both parties.
