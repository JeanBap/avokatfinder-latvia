Nomas Līgums

A nomas līgums is a Latvian lease or rental agreement — the contract governing the rights and obligations of a landlord and tenant in respect of residential or commercial property.

The nomas līgums (rental agreement) is the primary instrument regulating landlord-tenant relationships in Latvia. Residential tenancy law is governed by the Dzīvojamo telpu īres likums (Residential Tenancy Act), while commercial leases are primarily governed by the Civil Law.

For residential tenancies, the nomas līgums must specify the leased premises, the monthly rent (īres maksa), utility obligations, the security deposit (drošības nauda — capped at two months' rent for indefinite tenancies), and the notice period. Oral agreements are permissible but strongly inadvisable.

Latvian residential tenancy law grants significant security of tenure. A landlord may terminate a tenancy before expiry only on specific grounds including non-payment of rent, serious breach of contract, or the landlord's own genuine need for the property. Eviction without a court order is unlawful.

Under Latvian law, a nomas līgums exceeding two years (or any indefinite residential tenancy) should be registered in the Zemesgrāmata to be effective against third parties — particularly important if the property is sold mid-tenancy, as an unregistered long-term lease may not bind the new owner.

Common Mistake: Relying on an oral nomas līgums for residential lettings in Latvia. While technically valid, an oral agreement provides minimal protection in disputes — a written nomas līgums signed by both parties is essential for enforcing rent, deposit, and notice obligations.
Expert Tip: Register any nomas līgums exceeding two years in the Zemesgrāmata. Registration protects the tenant against eviction if the property is sold, and protects the landlord against the tenant claiming the lease is not binding on a new owner.

Foreign investors acquiring Latvian residential property subject to existing nomas līgumi should verify whether they are registered in the Zemesgrāmata and review the existing terms before completing the purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What rights does a Latvian residential tenant have on termination?

Under the Dzīvojamo telpu īres likums, a tenant cannot be evicted except on specific statutory grounds — such as non-payment of rent, serious breach, or the landlord's genuine personal need for the property. Eviction requires a court order in all cases.

Is a security deposit required under Latvian tenancy law?

A security deposit (drošības nauda) is not legally mandatory but is standard practice. For indefinite residential tenancies, the deposit is capped at two months' rent. It must be returned within one month of the tenancy ending, minus any lawful deductions.

Does a Latvian lease survive the sale of the property?

A registered long-term nomas līgums (registered in the Zemesgrāmata) is binding on any new owner. An unregistered long-term lease may not bind a purchaser who acquires without notice, so tenants in long-term arrangements should ensure registration.
