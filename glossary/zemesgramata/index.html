Zemesgrāmata

Zemesgrāmata is the Latvian Land Book — the official state register recording all real property ownership rights, mortgages, easements, and encumbrances in Latvia.

The Zemesgrāmata is maintained by the Latvian courts (rajona tiesu zemesgrāmatu nodaļas) and is the definitive legal record of real property ownership in Latvia. Registration in the Zemesgrāmata is constitutive — ownership and other real rights vest only upon registration, not upon signing the purchase contract.

The Land Book is organised by property entries (nekustamā īpašuma ieraksti), each containing sections for property description, ownership, and encumbrances. The public portal (kadastrs.lv) allows free searches of cadastral information; a Zemesgrāmata extract (izraksts) is obtainable from the court or online.

On submission of a valid pirkuma līgums, the Zemesgrāmata places an annotation (atzīme) on the property entry, protecting the buyer's priority during the processing period. Any subsequent applications filed after the atzīme ranks lower in priority.

Before any purchase, buyers should obtain a fresh Zemesgrāmata extract to verify ownership, check for mortgages (hipotēka), easements (servitūts), and any restrictions on alienation or use. This extract is the primary due diligence document in Latvian transactions.

Common Mistake: Relying on the cadastral map at kadastrs.lv as a substitute for a Zemesgrāmata extract. The cadastre shows physical boundaries and cadastral values; the Land Book shows legal ownership and encumbrances. Both are needed for complete due diligence.
Expert Tip: Use the authenticated portal manas.zemesgramata.lv to pull a fresh Zemesgrāmata extract the morning of your signing. Extracts are available within seconds and reflect the current state of the register.

The Zemesgrāmata also records property boundaries and cadastral changes, working in conjunction with the State Land Service (Valsts zemes dienests — VZD) which maintains the cadastral map. Boundaries visible on the cadastral map may not always match the Land Book description — both must be checked.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Latvian Zemesgrāmata publicly accessible?

The Zemesgrāmata is accessible to authenticated users via manas.zemesgramata.lv. Some basic information is available publicly via kadastrs.lv. Full extracts showing all encumbrances require authentication or a formal application to the district court.

What is an atzīme in the Latvian Zemesgrāmata?

An atzīme is an annotation placed on a Zemesgrāmata entry when an application for registration has been received but not yet processed. It protects the applicant's priority against any competing applications filed subsequently.

How long does Latvian Land Book registration take?

Standard registration in the Zemesgrāmata takes approximately five to seven working days after submission of the correctly certified documents. Expedited registration within one working day is available for an additional state fee.
