Zemes Noma

Zemes noma is the Latvian land lease — an arrangement where a building owner leases the land beneath their building from a separate landowner, a common legacy of Soviet-era property restitution in Latvia.

The zemes noma phenomenon is unique to post-Soviet Baltic states and is particularly prevalent in Latvia, especially in Riga. During property restitution in the 1990s, land was returned to pre-war owners or their heirs while apartment buildings on that land were privatised separately. The result: building owners pay an annual land lease fee to land owners they may never have chosen as counterparties.

Zemes noma relationships are governed by the Civil Law and the Apartment Ownership Law. The land lease fee is typically set as a percentage (up to 6%) of the cadastral value of the land. This annual cost is a significant factor in the total cost of ownership for affected apartments.

The Latvian government has sought to resolve the zemes noma issue through compulsory purchase legislation allowing apartment owners to buy the underlying land. However, disputes over pricing have meant many relationships persist, creating ongoing financial obligations and uncertainty for apartment buyers.

Before purchasing a Latvian apartment in Riga or other urban centres, buyers must check whether the land beneath the building is owned by the condominium community or held under zemes noma by a third party. This information is visible in the Zemesgrāmata for the land parcel.

Common Mistake: Buying a Riga apartment without checking whether the building sits on owner-occupied land or land held under zemes noma. An annual land lease fee of several hundred euros can materially affect the property's investment returns and resale value.
Expert Tip: Check both the apartment's Zemesgrāmata entry and the separate land parcel entry before buying. If zemes noma exists, negotiate a price reduction reflecting the ongoing obligation, and consult a Latvian lawyer about compulsory purchase options.

Apartments subject to ongoing zemes noma obligations are less attractive to buyers and lenders than those where the condominium owns the land outright. The annual fee adds to holding costs and creates counterparty risk over the long term.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is zemes noma in Latvia?

Zemes noma is a land lease arrangement arising from Soviet-era restitution in Latvia. Land was returned to pre-war owners while apartment buildings on that land were privatised separately — resulting in building owners paying annual fees to land owners.

How is the zemes noma fee calculated in Latvia?

The annual zemes noma fee is typically set at a percentage of the cadastral value of the land parcel, up to a maximum of 6% per annum. The exact rate is negotiated or set under Latvian legislation governing restituted land.

Can Latvian apartment owners buy the land beneath their building?

Yes. Latvian legislation provides compulsory purchase rights enabling apartment owners to acquire the underlying land from restituted land owners at a regulated price. The process is administered through the courts and has been used to resolve many historical zemes noma arrangements.
