Kadastra Vērtība

Kadastra vērtība is the Latvian cadastral value — the official state-assessed value of real property used as the basis for calculating annual real estate tax in Latvia.

Every property in Latvia is assigned a kadastra vērtība (cadastral value) by the State Land Service (Valsts zemes dienests — VZD). This value is recalculated periodically and is typically below market value, reflecting a standardised mass appraisal rather than an individual market valuation.

The kadastra vērtība is the tax base for Nekustamā īpašuma nodoklis (real estate tax). The standard tax rate is 1.5% of the cadastral value annually for residential property, though municipalities have some discretion to set rates within prescribed bands.

The cadastral value is also used as a reference point in certain stamp duty and state fee calculations. It is publicly available for any property via the kadastrs.lv portal without registration. Buyers should compare the cadastral value against the purchase price to understand the relative position.

Latvia plans periodic cadastral value revaluations to bring assessed values closer to market levels. When values increase significantly, owners' annual tax liabilities rise accordingly — buyers should factor potential revaluation risk into long-term ownership cost projections.

Common Mistake: Assuming the kadastra vērtība reflects the property's market value. The cadastral value is a standardised tax base, often significantly below market value. Banks and buyers should always commission an independent novērtējums for accurate market valuation.
Expert Tip: Check the latest kadastra vērtība on kadastrs.lv before making an offer — it indicates the annual tax liability and provides a rough floor below which the declared sale price might trigger FKTK or tax authority scrutiny.

The kadastra vērtība should not be confused with the market value or the appraisal value used by banks for mortgage purposes. Lenders commission independent certified valuations (novērtējums) rather than relying on the cadastral figure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the real estate tax rate in Latvia?

Latvia levies Nekustamā īpašuma nodoklis at 1.5% of the kadastra vērtība annually for most residential property. Municipalities may adjust rates within permitted bands. Agricultural land and certain social housing have different rate structures.

Where can I find the kadastra vērtība for a Latvian property?

The kadastra vērtība is publicly accessible for any property in Latvia via the kadastrs.lv portal operated by the Valsts zemes dienests. No registration is required for a basic search.

Does the cadastral value affect Latvian property purchase costs?

The kadastra vērtība can affect certain state fees and stamp duties where the declared purchase price is below the cadastral value. Authorities may use the higher of the two as the basis for assessment.
